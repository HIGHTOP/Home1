3D modeling is the process of developing a representation of any object in three dimensions with a specialized software. Nowadays more and more companies turn to 3D modeling because it can save resources (time and money) for them. For example, 75% of the IKEA catalog is now done in 3D. These are not pictures of real rooms, but 3D models created by professionals on a computer. As 3D printing becomes cheap and available everywhere, it also makes sense to develop modeling skills to easily bring ideas to life. With a 3D printer and on-demand manufacturing, you can turn ideas into real, physical objects in just a few hours. These could be toys, fun objects, household items or even replacement parts. The democratization of design allows people to be creative in 3D.
